Samuel Leder is a researcher in the field of computational design and digital fabrication, investigating the codesign of collective robotic construction systems. Samuel is currently completing his doctoral thesis at the Institute for Computational Design and Construction (ICD) within the Cluster of Excellence Integrative Computational Design and Construction for Architecture (IntCDC) at the University of Stuttgart. He is currently a Visiting Lecturer in the Department of Design Tech, part of the Design Tech Innovation Fellowship.

Samuel's work has been published in various academic venues, including Advanced Science, Automation in Construction, ACADIA, and eCAADe, and exhibited at various venues, including ARS Electronica (Linz), Bureau Europa (Maastricht) and HumBase (Stuttgart). Samuel earned a B.Des. in Architecture and a B.A.Sc. in Systems Science and Engineering from Washington University in St. Louis and a M.Sc. in Architecture through the Integrative Technologies and Architectural Design Research (ITECH) program at the University of Stuttgart.
