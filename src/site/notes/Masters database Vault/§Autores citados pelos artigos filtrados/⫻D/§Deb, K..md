After receiving Bachelor's degree in Mechanical Engineering from IIT Kharagpur in India in 1985, I worked with Engineers India Limited in New Delhi for a couple of years and then went for higher studies to USA. After receiving my MS and PhD degrees from University of Alabama, I worked at University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign for almost two years. Then, I returned to India and joined IIT Kanpur as an assistant professor in December 1992. I become a full professor in 1999. I held Endowed Chair Professor positions at IIT Kanpur. I have also worked at other universities abroad (University of Dortmund, Germany; ETH Zurich; University of Karlsruhe, Germany; University of Birmingham, UK; Nanyang Technological University Singapore; Aalto University School of Economics, Finland).  
  
Currently, I am Koenig Endowed Chair Professor at the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering, Michigan State University, East Lansing, USA. I also have joint appointments as professor in Department of Mechanical Engineering and in Department of Computer Science and Engineering at MSU.  
  
My main research interests include development and application of computer-aided algorithms for solving different types of optimization problems. My methodologies are adopted by commercial software industries (Red Ceder Technologies, iSIGHT, Vanderplatts Inc., Estico, and others) and used by major industries around the world. More about my research can be found at https://www.egr.msu.edu/~kdeb/.  
  
My Computational Optimization and Innovation (COIN) Laboratory at Michigan State University promotes research and application on development and application of optimization methods for different practical problems. More information can be found at https://www.coin-laboratory.com.  
  
Specialties: Optimal design, data mining, multi-criterion optimization, knowledge discovery, games. artificial intelligence, machine learning.
