Tyson Hosmer
Senior Associate Researcher at Zaha Hadid Architects
Profile
Tyson Hosmer is an Associate at Zaha Hadid Architects working with
the ZH Code group. His research is focused on embedding localized
intelligence in nonlinear organization systems applied in a design
process. Currently his research focuses on the application of machine
learning with agent based systems.
Education
Tyson holds a Masters Degree from the Architectural Association
Design Research Lab and a Bachelors Degree in Architecture from
Virginia Tech. He has over 10 years of experience as a designer,
software developer and researcher previously working in several
architecture offices including Axi:Ome, Asymptote Architecture,
Kokkugia, and Cecil Balmond Studio where he was the research
director for several years. He has been teaching at the Design
Research Lab since 2011. He has taught in the AA Emergent
Technologies program and UCL Bartlett as well as teaching workshops
and presenting work in the UK, Europe, and U.S.
