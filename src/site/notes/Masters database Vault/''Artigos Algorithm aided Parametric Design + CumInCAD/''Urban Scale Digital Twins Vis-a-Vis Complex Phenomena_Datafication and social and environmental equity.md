Justificativa:
  - '"An important reorientation concerning the role of the urban scale digital twins in urban planning is that from technical to the socio-technical perspectives. The debates on Smart Cities often focus on technical issues, neglecting the social issues concerning urban planning. At the core of the paper is the idea that in order to combine environmental equity and social equity, it is pivotal to bring together the social and the technical viewpoints of urban planning. This can become possible through the adoption of socio-technical perspectives."'
  - '"Citizens can download and upload data enhancing in this way social equity and participatory design methods "'
  - They suggest that the notion that should replace the term ‘digital twin’ should be “cyber–physical–social system with coupled properties” (Tomko, Winter, 2019, p. 396). They argue that this shift is related to a recognition of the fact that digital models do not function exclusively as a “passive reflection of a mirror”"
  - '" Batty also argues that “one of the quests in city modelling is to merge social and economic processes with the built environment and to link functional and physical processes to socio-economic representations."'
  - '"This means that the ways in which urban scale digital twins function often neglect the importance of social interactions, competition and cooperation, social norms, laws and regulations, culture, history, politics, democracy, human rights, ethics, and essential non-material qualities"'
