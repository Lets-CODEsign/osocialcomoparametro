Justificativa:
  - The goal of this paper is to explore a sustainable strategy to address the conflict between urban sprawl and industrial conservation in Alexandria, Sydney. Through the application of a sequential evolutionary simulation, the presented research proposes a potential mixed-use scheme to rejuvenate the existing industrial district of Alexandria in an integrative manner without necessitating its destruction.
  -  The aim of the simulations isto address the complexity of the site without the need to abstract the formulation of thedesign problem, allowing for each simulation to tackle a unique set of fitness functionsat different scales, in which the output of the first simulation (network and plotdivisions) translates as the input for the second simulation (block typology andprogrammatic distribution). Additionally, to achieve a better understanding ofAlexandria’s urban network, accessibility and spatial configurations, Space Syntaxtheory (Hillier and Hanson, 1984) is integrated within the simulation as both an analyticand generative tool
