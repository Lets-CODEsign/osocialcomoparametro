Pei holds a Bachelor's degree in Architecture from UTS and a Master's degree in Architecture from USYD. He currently holds a post-study Working Visa 485 and has received an invitation from the DHA for the 189 Permanent Residency, awaiting approval for the 189 PR visa. Therefore, he can live and work in Australia permanently without visa restrictions.  
  
Prior to graduation, he had three competition experiences and internship experiences at design firms. His course design in the first year of his graduate studies was selected by his instructor to participate in a parametric design competition and successfully published an academic paper, "An Alternative Model for Urban Renewal: A Generative Approach to the (Re)-Development of Xian Village," at the CAADRIA 2022 conference.Adaptable and highly collaborative, skilled in team communication and task arrangement in both academic and professional settings, successfully delivering design solutions for multiple team projects.  
  
Skilled in 2D/3D Modeling and Visualization including Revit, Archicad, Rhino, Adobe sets (Illustrator, Photoshop, Indesign), and Rendering (Enscape). I leverage these tools to translate abstract ideas into tangible and innovative architectural solutions. Particularly interested in physical model-making and architectural photography, while excelling in Lightroom post-production.  
  
Design Philosophy:  
The birth of a building signifies not only the completion of a human construction but also the transformation of its surrounding space, redefining the spirit of that place, an impact that will endure as long as the building stands. Our pursuit of architectural design is predicated on this: design should not merely serve our current era but seek its timeless, enduring vitality, creating true integrity in architecture.
